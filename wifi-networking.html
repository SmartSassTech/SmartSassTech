Understanding Your Home Network

Router Basics

Your router creates the Wi-Fi network in your home. It connects to your modem (from your internet provider) and broadcasts the wireless signal that your devices use to get online.

Wi-Fi Extenders

Have dead zones in your home? Wi-Fi extenders (also called repeaters) boost your signal to reach farther. Mesh systems provide even better coverage throughout larger homes.

Speed Matters

Internet speed is measured in Mbps (megabits per second). For basic browsing and email, 25 Mbps is fine. For streaming and multiple devices, 100+ Mbps is better.

TIP: Place your router in a central location, away from walls and metal objects. Restart it occasionally (unplug for 30 seconds) to keep things running smoothly!
