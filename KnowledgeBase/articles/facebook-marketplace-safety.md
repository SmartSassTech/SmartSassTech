Buying and selling locally on Facebook Marketplace can save you money and help you find great deals. But just like meeting a stranger at a garage sale, you need to be careful. This guide will show you how to use Marketplace safely so you can shop with confidence.

**Estimated Time to Complete:** 20 minutes to read, ongoing practice

**What You'll Need:**

* A Facebook account
* Your smartphone or computer
* A safe public meeting place in mind (we'll help you choose one)

---

## Understanding Facebook Marketplace

Facebook Marketplace is like an online garage sale or classified ads section. People in your area list items they want to sell, and you can browse, message sellers, and arrange to buy things locally.

**What makes it different from a store:**

* You're buying from regular people, not businesses
* You usually meet in person to exchange money and items
* No guarantees or return policies like at a store
* Prices are often negotiable

**Why people use it:**

* Find good deals on furniture, electronics, clothes, and more
* Sell things you no longer need
* Shop locally without shipping costs
* See items in person before buying

---

### Step 1: Finding Facebook Marketplace

#### On Your Phone

1. Open the **Facebook** app (blue icon with white "f").
2. Look at the bottom of your screen for a row of icons.
3. Tap the icon that looks like a **small storefront** or **shopping bag** (usually in the middle).
4. You're now in Marketplace.

#### On Your Computer

1. Go to **facebook.com** and sign in.
2. Look at the left side of your screen.
3. Click **Marketplace** (looks like a small storefront icon).
4. You're now in Marketplace.

**If you don't see Marketplace:** Not all Facebook accounts have access to it. This is normal and not something you did wrong. Facebook decides who can use certain features.

---

### Step 2: Browsing Safely

#### How to Look at Items

1. On the Marketplace home screen, you'll see items for sale near you.
2. Scroll down to see more.
3. Tap or click on any item to see details, photos, and the seller's asking price.

#### What to Look For

**Good signs:**

* Clear, well-lit photos showing the item from multiple angles
* Detailed description including the item's condition
* Reasonable price (not too low to be believable)
* Seller has a real-looking Facebook profile with friends and posts

**Warning signs:**

* Only one blurry photo
* Price seems too good to be true (brand new iPhone for $100)
* Description says "must sell today" or "cash only, no questions"
* Seller's Facebook profile is brand new with no friends or posts
* Seller asks you to pay before meeting

---

### Step 3: Messaging a Seller Safely

When you find something you're interested in, you'll need to message the seller.

#### Starting a Conversation

1. On the item's page, click or tap **Message seller** or **Is this available?**
2. Facebook opens a private message conversation.
3. Type your question in the message box at the bottom.
4. Tap or click the **send arrow**.

#### Safe Questions to Ask

* "Is this item still available?"
* "What condition is it in? Any scratches or damage?"
* "Where would you like to meet?"
* "When are you available to meet?"

#### Things to Never Share in Messages

* Your home address
* Your phone number (until you've decided to meet)
* Your credit card or bank account information
* Your social security number

**Why this matters:** Scammers collect personal information to steal identities or commit fraud. Keep conversations in Facebook Messenger until you're ready to meet.

---

### Safety Tip: Red Flags to Watch For

Walk away immediately if the seller:

* Asks you to pay before meeting (via Venmo, PayPal, Zelle, gift cards, wire transfer)
* Won't meet in a public place
* Pressures you to decide quickly ("Someone else wants it, you need to decide now")
* Asks you to click a link to "verify your account" or "pay securely"
* Offers to ship the item when the listing says local pickup
* Has a sob story about why they need money urgently

**If something feels wrong, trust your instincts.** It's better to miss a deal than to get scammed or put yourself in danger.

---

### Step 4: Arranging a Safe Meeting Place

Never invite a stranger to your home, and never go to a stranger's home. Always meet in public.

#### Best Meeting Places

**Police station parking lots:**

* Many police stations have designated "safe exchange zones"
* Cameras are watching
* Officers are nearby
* Scammers avoid these locations

**Busy public places during daytime:**

* Bank lobbies (cameras and security)
* Coffee shop parking lots (Starbucks, Dunkin')
* Grocery store parking lots
* Shopping mall entrances

**Why these work:** Lots of people around, security cameras, and well-lit areas make everyone safer.

#### Places to Avoid

* Your home or the seller's home
* Empty parking lots
* Secluded areas
* Meeting after dark

#### How to Suggest a Meeting Place

In your message, say:

"I'd like to meet at the Starbucks on Main Street. Does Saturday at 2 PM work for you?"

**If they refuse to meet in public, end the conversation.** No legitimate seller will object to a safe meeting place.

---

### Step 5: Preparing for the Meeting

#### Before You Go

1. **Tell someone where you're going:**
   * Tell a friend or family member: "I'm meeting someone from Facebook Marketplace at [location] at [time]."
   * Share the seller's name and profile link.
   * Text them when you arrive and when you leave.

2. **Bring a friend if possible:**
   * Two people are safer than one.
   * A second person can help inspect the item.

3. **Bring exact cash:**
   * Count out the agreed-upon amount at home.
   * Put it in your pocket (not visible).
   * Don't bring your entire wallet or extra cash.

4. **Bring your phone:**
   * Fully charged.
   * Keep it accessible.

#### What to Bring for Specific Items

**Electronics (phones, tablets, laptops):**

* Bring a charger to test if it powers on
* Check for cracks, scratches, password locks

**Furniture:**

* Bring a tape measure if size matters
* Bring help to lift and transport it

**Clothing:**

* You generally can't try things on at public meetings
* Know your size and ask for measurements

---

### Step 6: At the Meeting

#### What to Do

1. **Arrive a few minutes early.**
   * Park in a visible spot.
   * Wait in your car if you're early.

2. **Inspect the item carefully:**
   * Does it match the photos?
   * Turn it on if it's electronic.
   * Look for damage or wear.
   * Ask questions if something seems different.

3. **Decide if you still want it:**
   * It's okay to change your mind.
   * It's okay to negotiate the price if the item isn't as described.

4. **Exchange money and item:**
   * Hand over cash when you receive the item.
   * Count any change carefully.

5. **Leave:**
   * Text your friend or family member that you're done.
   * Drive away.

#### If Something Feels Wrong

* You don't have to go through with it.
* Say, "I've changed my mind, thank you anyway."
* Walk back to your car and leave.
* Don't feel bad. Your safety is more important than being polite.

---

### Step 7: After the Purchase

#### Mark the Item as Sold (If You're the Buyer)

1. Go back to the item listing.
2. You may see an option to **Mark as picked up** or **Leave a rating**.
3. This helps other buyers know the seller is reliable.

#### What to Do If You're Happy

* Leave a positive review if Facebook prompts you.
* Save the seller's profile if you'd buy from them again.

#### What to Do If There's a Problem

**If the item doesn't work:**

* Facebook Marketplace has no buyer protection for in-person sales.
* Contact the seller politely and ask if they'll make it right.
* If they refuse, you likely can't get your money back.
* This is why inspecting items before buying is so important.

**If you were scammed:**

* Report the seller to Facebook: Go to their profile → three dots (...) → **Find support or report profile**.
* Report to your local police if you lost a significant amount of money.
* Warn friends so they don't fall for the same scam.

### Selling on Marketplace Safely

If you're selling items instead of buying, the same safety rules apply.

#### Listing an Item

1. Open Facebook Marketplace.
2. Tap or click **Create new listing** or the **+ (plus) icon**.
3. Take clear photos of your item from multiple angles.
4. Write a description: what it is, condition, size, any flaws.
5. Set a fair price (search similar items to see what others charge).
6. Choose **Local pickup only** (never offer to ship to strangers).
7. Click **Post** or **Publish**.

#### Communicating with Buyers

* Respond to messages promptly.
* Answer questions honestly.
* Suggest a public meeting place.
* Never give out your home address.

#### At the Meeting (as a Seller)

* Meet in public just like when you're buying.
* Bring a friend.
* Bring your phone.
* Have the item ready to show.
* Accept cash only (no checks, no Venmo before meeting).
* Don't let strangers come to your home to "test out" furniture or electronics.

### Common Marketplace Scams

#### The Overpayment Scam

**What happens:** Buyer sends you a check for more than the asking price and asks you to refund the difference.

**The truth:** The check is fake. It bounces, but you've already sent them money.

**How to avoid it:** Accept cash only. Never accept checks from strangers.

#### The Shipping Scam

**What happens:** Someone messages you about an item, claims they can't meet locally, and asks you to ship it. They send you a fake payment confirmation.

**The truth:** They never paid. You ship the item and lose it.

**How to avoid it:** Only sell to people who can meet you in person locally.

#### The "Verify Your Account" Scam

**What happens:** Buyer asks you to click a link to "verify your account" before they'll buy from you.

**The truth:** The link steals your Facebook password.

**How to avoid it:** Never click links in Marketplace messages. Facebook doesn't require verification to sell.

#### The Fake Payment App Scam

**What happens:** Buyer shows you a screenshot of a Venmo/PayPal payment and asks you to hand over the item. The screenshot is fake.

**The truth:** No money was actually sent.

**How to avoid it:** Use cash only. Check your bank account or payment app yourself before handing over items.

### Protecting Your Facebook Account

Marketplace scammers sometimes try to steal your Facebook login.

#### Keep Your Account Safe

1. **Use a strong password:**
   * At least 12 characters
   * Mix of letters, numbers, and symbols
   * Not used on other accounts

2. **Enable two-factor authentication:**
   * Facebook sends a code to your phone when you log in
   * Settings → Security and Login → Two-Factor Authentication → Turn On

3. **Don't click suspicious links:**
   * Scammers send fake Facebook messages with links
   * Never click links asking you to "verify your account"
   * Go directly to facebook.com instead

4. **Review login activity:**
   * Settings → Security and Login → Where You're Logged In
   * Log out of devices you don't recognize

### When Marketplace Works Well

Despite the risks, many people use Marketplace successfully every day.

**Good uses:**

* Buying used furniture at a fraction of retail price
* Selling baby clothes and toys your kids outgrew
* Finding local deals on exercise equipment, books, or home decor
* Connecting with neighbors for free items they're giving away

**Bad uses:**

* Buying expensive electronics from strangers (too easy to get scammed)
* Selling items worth over $500 (too risky to carry that much cash)
* Anything requiring shipping to strangers

---

### Alternatives to Marketplace

If Marketplace doesn't feel safe or isn't working for you:

* **Craigslist:** Similar to Marketplace but not connected to Facebook
* **OfferUp:** Another local buying/selling app
* **Nextdoor:** Neighborhood app for local sales (often friendlier)
* **Garage sales:** Traditional in-person shopping in your neighborhood
* **Consignment shops:** Sell clothes, furniture, and items through a store
* **Donation:** Give items to Goodwill, Salvation Army, or local charities

### Common Questions

**Q: How do I know if a seller is trustworthy?**  
A: Check their Facebook profile. Do they have friends, posts, and photos going back months or years? If their profile is brand new with no activity, be cautious. Also check if they have reviews from other Marketplace buyers (shown on their profile).

**Q: Can I get my money back if something goes wrong?**  
A: No. Facebook Marketplace doesn't offer buyer protection for local in-person sales. This is why inspecting items carefully before paying is critical. Only Facebook Checkout purchases (shipped items with tracking) have some protection.

**Q: Is it safe to give a seller my phone number?**  
A: It's generally okay once you've decided to meet, but keep all early communication in Facebook Messenger. Your phone number is less private than you think, but scammers prefer Messenger for schemes anyway.

**Q: Someone wants to pay with Venmo. Is that safe?**  
A: Only if they pay BEFORE you meet and you verify the money is in your account. However, cash is safer because payment apps can be reversed. If a buyer insists on Venmo, verify the payment arrived before handing over the item.

**Q: What if the seller wants to meet at their house?**  
A: Politely suggest a public place instead: "I prefer to meet in public. How about the Starbucks on Main Street?" If they refuse, end the conversation. It's not worth the risk.

**Q: How do I know what price to charge for something I'm selling?**  
A: Search Marketplace for similar items and see what they're listed for. Price yours competitively. Remember, used items sell for 40-70% less than new ones, depending on condition.

**Q: Can I return something I bought on Marketplace?**  
A: No. All sales are final. The seller has no obligation to take it back or refund you. This is why inspecting items thoroughly before buying is so important.

**Q: Someone keeps messaging me about an item I'm selling, but they're not actually buying. What should I do?**  
A: Some people are just browsing or asking questions with no intent to buy. It's okay to stop responding after a few messages if they're not serious. You can also hide or delete your listing if you get too many time-wasters.

**Q: I was scammed. What should I do?**  
A: Report the person on Facebook (their profile → three dots → Find support or report profile). If you lost a significant amount of money, file a police report. Warn your friends. Unfortunately, recovering money from Marketplace scams is very difficult.

**Q: Is Marketplace only for used items?**  
A: No. Some people sell new items they received as gifts or items they bought but never used. Small businesses sometimes sell new products too. Just verify the item is actually new if that's what you're paying for.

**Q: Can I haggle on the price?**  
A: Yes, negotiating is common on Marketplace. If an item is listed for $50, you can message: "Would you accept $40?" Many sellers price items a bit higher expecting to negotiate. Be polite and reasonable.

**Q: What does "porch pickup" mean?**  
A: The seller leaves the item on their porch and you pick it up without meeting face-to-face. You usually pay via Venmo or leave cash. This became popular during COVID. Only do this with sellers who have good reviews.

### Quick Safety Checklist

Before every Marketplace purchase or sale:

* **Do** meet in a busy, public place during daytime
* **Do** tell someone where you're going and when
* **Do** inspect items carefully before paying
* **Do** trust your instincts—walk away if something feels wrong
* **Do** use cash only
* **Don't** give out your home address
* **Don't** go to someone's home or invite them to yours
* **Don't** click links in Marketplace messages
* **Don't** pay before meeting and inspecting the item
* **Don't** feel pressured to buy something you're unsure about

---

Buying and selling locally can save you money and help you find unique items you won't see in stores. With these safety steps, you can use Facebook Marketplace confidently. The key is staying alert, meeting in public, and listening to your instincts. Most people on Marketplace are honest neighbors trying to sell things they no longer need. By following these guidelines, you'll have positive experiences while staying safe.
