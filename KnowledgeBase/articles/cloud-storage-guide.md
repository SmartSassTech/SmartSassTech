Learn how to safely store your files online and access them from any device, anywhere.

## What Is Cloud Storage?

Cloud storage means saving your files on the internet instead of (or in addition to) your computer's hard drive.

**Think of it like:** A storage unit you can access from anywhere. Instead of keeping everything in your house (computer), you rent space in a facility (the cloud) that you can visit anytime, from anywhere.

**The "cloud" is:** Physical servers (powerful computers) owned by companies like Google, Apple, Microsoft, and others. When you save to the cloud, your files are stored on their computers in secure data centers.

## Why Use Cloud Storage?

Cloud storage solves many common problems:

## Access Files Anywhere

* Open your files from any device with internet
* Start work on your laptop, finish on your phone
* No need to email files to yourself

## Automatic Backup

* Files are protected if your computer crashes
* Phone stolen? Your photos are safe in the cloud
* Hard drive fails? Your documents are still accessible

## Easy Sharing

* Share files with others via a link
* Collaborate on documents in real-time
* Send large files without email attachment limits

## Free Up Space

* Store files in the cloud instead of on your device
* Especially useful for phones with limited storage
* Keep thousands of photos without filling your phone

## Popular Cloud Storage Services

Each service has its own strengths and works best with certain devices.

## Google Drive

* **Free storage:** 15GB (shared across Gmail, Photos, Drive)
* **Paid plans:** Start at $1.99/month for 100GB
* **Best for:** Android users, Google Workspace users, collaboration
* **Features:** Real-time document editing (Docs, Sheets, Slides), easy sharing
* **Access:** drive.google.com, mobile apps (iOS/Android), desktop app

## iCloud

* **Free storage:** 5GB
* **Paid plans:** Start at $0.99/month for 50GB
* **Best for:** Apple users (iPhone, iPad, Mac)
* **Features:** Automatic photo backup, seamless Apple device sync
* **Access:** icloud.com, built into Apple devices

## Microsoft OneDrive

* **Free storage:** 5GB
* **Paid plans:** Start at $1.99/month for 100GB (or included with Microsoft 365)
* **Best for:** Windows users, Microsoft Office users
* **Features:** Office integration, automatic PC backup
* **Access:** onedrive.com, built into Windows, mobile apps

## Dropbox

* **Free storage:** 2GB
* **Paid plans:** Start at $11.99/month for 2TB
* **Best for:** Power users, businesses, file syncing
* **Features:** Excellent sync, file versioning, team collaboration
* **Access:** dropbox.com, desktop and mobile apps

## Amazon Photos

* **Free storage:** 5GB (unlimited photo storage with Amazon Prime)
* **Best for:** Amazon Prime members, photographers
* **Features:** Unlimited full-resolution photo storage (Prime), automatic backup
* **Access:** amazon.com/photos, mobile apps

**Which should you choose?** Start with the one that works best with your devices:

* **iPhone/Mac:** iCloud
* **Android/Chromebook:** Google Drive
* **Windows/Microsoft Office:** OneDrive
* **Any device:** Google Drive or Dropbox

## How Cloud Storage Works

## The Basics

1. **Sign up** for a cloud storage account (usually free to start)
2. **Upload files** through website, app, or automatic sync
3. **Files are stored** on the company's servers
4. **Access files** from any device by logging into your account

## Two Ways to Use Cloud Storage

### Option 1: Upload/Download (Website/App)

* Go to the website or open the app
* Manually upload files you want to store
* Download files when you need them
* Files exist in the cloud AND on your device (until you delete from device)

### Option 2: Sync Folder (Desktop App)

* Install the desktop app on your computer
* A special folder appears on your computer (like "Google Drive" or "Dropbox")
* Files you put in this folder automatically upload to the cloud
* Changes sync automatically across all your devices
* Files exist in the cloud AND on your computer simultaneously

## Getting Started with Cloud Storage

## Step 1: Create an Account

**For Google Drive:**

1. Go to **drive.google.com**
2. Click **Go to Google Drive**
3. Sign in with your Google account (or create one)
4. You're ready to upload files

**For iCloud:**

1. Already set up if you have an iPhone/Mac
2. Go to **Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud** on iPhone
3. Or **System Settings > Apple ID > iCloud** on Mac
4. Turn on iCloud Drive

**For OneDrive:**

1. Already built into Windows 10/11
2. Click the **cloud icon** in your taskbar
3. Sign in with your Microsoft account
4. Or go to **onedrive.com**

## Step 2: Upload Your First File

**From a website:**

1. Click the **Upload** button (usually a + or ↑ icon)
2. Select **File** or **Folder**
3. Choose what to upload from your computer
4. Wait for the upload to complete

**From mobile app:**

1. Open the app (Google Drive, iCloud, etc.)
2. Tap the **+** button
3. Select **Upload** or **Upload Photos**
4. Choose files from your device
5. Files upload automatically

## Step 3: Access Your Files

* **On web:** Go to the website and log in
* **On phone:** Open the app
* **On computer:** Open the sync folder or go to the website

## Organizing Files in the Cloud

Use the same organization strategies as your computer:

## Create Folders

* Click **New Folder** or **+ > Folder**
* Name it descriptively
* Drag files into folders to organize

**Example structure:**

```text
My Drive/
├── Work/
│   ├── Projects/
│   ├── Reports/
├── Personal/
│   ├── Photos/
│   ├── Documents/
└── Shared with Me/
```

## Use Search

* All cloud services have powerful search
* Search by file name, content, or file type
* Faster than browsing when you have lots of files

## Sharing Files from the Cloud

One of the biggest benefits is easy sharing.

## Share a Single File

1. **Right-click the file** (or click the three dots)
2. Select **Share** or **Get link**
3. Choose permissions:
   * **View only** — they can see but not edit
   * **Comment** — they can add comments (not always available)
   * **Edit** — they can make changes
4. Choose who can access:
   * **Anyone with the link** — anyone who has the link can access
   * **Specific people** — only people you invite by email
5. **Copy the link** and send it via email, text, etc.

## Share a Folder

* Same process as sharing a file
* Everyone with access can see all files in the folder
* Great for team projects or family photo sharing

## Stop Sharing

* Go to the file or folder
* Click **Share** or **Manage access**
* Remove people or change the link settings
* Or delete the link entirely

**Important:** When you share with "Anyone with the link," anyone who gets that link can access the file—even if forwarded. Use "Specific people" for sensitive files.

## Keeping Your Files Safe in the Cloud

Cloud storage is generally very secure, but you should still take precautions.

## Use Strong Security

* **Strong password** — unique to this account (see our password guide)
* **Two-factor authentication** — enable it immediately (see our 2FA guide)
* **Log out on shared computers** — don't stay logged in on public devices

## Be Careful What You Upload

**Safe to upload:**

* Photos and videos
* Documents you've created
* Backup of important files

**Think twice before uploading:**

* Passwords or password lists
* Social Security cards or passport scans
* Private medical records
* Financial account information

**Better alternatives for sensitive documents:**

* Use an encrypted service like ProtonDrive
* Encrypt files before uploading
* Store locally on an encrypted external drive

## Review Shared Files Regularly

* Check what you've shared and with whom
* Remove access when no longer needed
* Delete old shared links you're not using

## Watch for Phishing

* Don't click suspicious links claiming to be from your cloud provider
* Cloud services will NEVER ask for your password via email
* Go directly to the website rather than clicking email links

## Understanding Storage Limits

## What Counts Against Your Storage

**Google Drive:**

* Files in Drive
* Gmail messages and attachments
* Google Photos (unless uploaded before June 2021)
* Does NOT count: Google Docs, Sheets, Slides

**iCloud:**

* Photos and videos
* Device backups
* iCloud Drive files
* iCloud Mail

**OneDrive:**

* Files in OneDrive
* Does NOT count (if using Microsoft 365): Office documents

## What Happens When You're Full

* **Can't upload** new files
* **Can't receive emails** (if email is included in storage)
* **Existing files are safe** — nothing gets deleted automatically
* **Get warnings** before you run out

## Managing Storage

**Check how much space you're using:**

* **Google:** drive.google.com/settings/storage
* **iCloud:** Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud
* **OneDrive:** onedrive.com, click settings gear, select Options

**Free up space:**

* Delete large files you don't need
* Empty trash (deleted files count until trash is emptied)
* Remove old email attachments (Gmail)
* Delete duplicate photos
* Manage device backups (iCloud)

**Upgrade storage if needed:**

* Usually $1-3/month for 50-100GB
* Worth it if you use the service regularly

## Automatic Backup Features

Many cloud services can automatically back up your devices.

## Google Photos Backup (Android/iPhone)

* Automatically backs up all photos and videos
* Free with 15GB limit (counts against Drive storage)
* Settings in Google Photos app > Settings > Backup

## iCloud Photo Library (iPhone/iPad/Mac)

* Automatically backs up photos across Apple devices
* Counts against iCloud storage limit
* Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > Photos

## OneDrive PC Backup (Windows)

* Backs up Desktop, Documents, and Pictures folders
* Settings in OneDrive app > Settings > Backup

**Recommendation:** Enable automatic photo backup on your phone. Losing photos is one of the most common regrets when phones are lost or broken.

## Accessing Files Offline

You can access cloud files even without internet.

## How Offline Access Works

* Download files or folders for offline use
* Access them without internet
* Changes sync when you reconnect to internet

## Enable Offline Access

**Google Drive:**

* Desktop: Install Drive for Desktop app
* Mobile: Tap the three dots > Make available offline

**Dropbox:**

* Mark files as "Available Offline" in settings
* Desktop app syncs automatically

**OneDrive:**

* Desktop app syncs automatically
* Mobile: Make files available offline in settings

**Storage consideration:** Offline files take up space on your device.

Cloud storage makes teamwork easier.

## Real-Time Editing

**Google Workspace (Drive, Docs, Sheets, Slides):**

* Multiple people can edit simultaneously
* See others' cursors as they type
* Changes save automatically

**Microsoft 365 (OneDrive, Word, Excel, PowerPoint):**

## Version History

Accidentally deleted something or need an older version?

* Right-click file > Version History
* See previous versions of the file
* Restore an old version if needed
* Usually keeps versions for 30-90 days

**Great for:**

* Recovering accidentally deleted content
* Seeing what changed and when
* Undoing unwanted edits

## Cloud Storage vs. Backup Services

Cloud storage and backup are related but different:

## Cloud Storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.)

* **Purpose:** Access files anywhere, share files, light backup
* **Selective:** You choose what to upload
* **Two-way sync:** Changes on any device sync everywhere
* **Easy access:** Files are readily available

## Backup Services (Backblaze, Carbonite, etc.)

* **Purpose:** Complete backup of your entire computer
* **Automatic:** Backs up everything on your computer
* **One-way:** Files go to backup, but restoring is manual
* **Recovery focused:** Designed for disaster recovery

**Best practice:** Use both:

* **Cloud storage** for files you actively work with
* **Backup service** for complete computer protection

## Common Cloud Storage Mistakes

## Mistake 1: Not Enabling 2FA

* Cloud accounts are high-value targets for hackers
* Always enable two-factor authentication
* See our 2FA guide

## Mistake 2: Sharing Too Broadly

* Don't use "Anyone with the link" for sensitive files
* Review and remove old shared links
* Use specific email sharing for private content

## Mistake 3: Only Having One Copy

* Cloud storage can fail or accounts can be hacked
* Follow the 3-2-1 backup rule (see our file organization guide)
* Don't rely solely on cloud storage

## Mistake 4: Not Checking Storage Regularly

* You don't want to run out of space unexpectedly
* Review storage monthly
* Delete old files or upgrade if needed

## Mistake 5: Uploading Unencrypted Sensitive Data

* Cloud providers can technically access your files
* Encrypt sensitive documents before uploading
* Or use encrypted cloud services for sensitive data

## Switching Cloud Services

Changed your mind? You can move to a different service.

## How to Transfer Files

1. **Download from old service:**
   * Select all files
   * Download as a zip file
   * Or use desktop sync folder

2. **Upload to new service:**
   * Upload the downloaded files
   * Or copy from one sync folder to another

**Easier option:** Some services offer migration tools:

* Google Takeout (export from Google)
* Import tools in some services

**Before switching:**

* Check who you've shared files with
* Update any shared links
* Inform collaborators of the change

## Quick Cloud Storage Checklist

* **Do** enable two-factor authentication
* **Do** back up important files to the cloud
* **Do** enable automatic photo backup on your phone
* **Do** check your storage usage regularly
* **Do** use specific sharing (not "Anyone with the link") for sensitive files
* **Don't** upload extremely sensitive data without encryption
* **Don't** share your account password with others
* **Don't** forget to empty trash to free up space
* **Don't** rely on cloud storage as your only backup
* **Don't** leave old shared links active indefinitely

## Getting the Most from Free Storage

If you're on a free plan, maximize your space:

### Google Drive (Maximizing Free Space)

* Use Google Docs, Sheets, Slides (don't count against storage)
* Manage Gmail storage (delete old emails with attachments)
* Adjust Google Photos quality settings

### iCloud (Maximizing Free Space)

* Turn off backup for apps you don't need backed up
* Delete old device backups
* Use Google Photos instead for photos (more free space)

### OneDrive (Maximizing Free Space)

* Use Office Online (doesn't count against storage)
* Turn off automatic camera upload if not needed

### Multiple Services Strategy

* Use different services for different purposes
* Photos in Google Photos (if Prime: Amazon Photos)
* Documents in OneDrive
* Collaboration in Google Drive
* Gets you 25-30GB free total
